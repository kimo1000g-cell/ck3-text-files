Achievement
Jump to navigationJump to search
This article has been verified for the current PC version (1.19) of the game.

Crusader Kings III has 188 different achievements, all using the Steam achievements mechanics. Achievements have no in-game effects; the achievement just pops up in-game, and is then added to the player's Steam profile after one more in-game day has passed.

The difficulty of achievements varies: some achievements are very easy (e.g. marrying another character), while some are much more difficult (e.g. restoring the Roman Empire).

Achievements can only be gained in single-player games. They can be earned while playing older versions of the game as long as necessary DLC was present in that version. Certain game rules will also disable achievements for the savegame: usually those which mostly make the game easier. If the Ruler Designer was used, achievements can only be obtained if the character didn't use more than 400 customization points are spent and only if the player starts the game as the newly-created character (a few achievements are explicitly blocked if starting as a custom ruler). Manually switching to another ruler, opening the game for multiplayer, or using console commands will block achievements for the current game. Since version 1.9, neither Ironman mode nor an unmodified checksum is required for achievements to remain enabled.

Please be aware that most of the hints and tips given on this page assume that all major DLC packs are installed and enabled. For the most part, if you do not have one of the DLCs enabled, the tips given here will work. However, some of the DLC packs (especially Roads to Power, Khans of the Steppe, and All Under Heaven, which add new government types with different mechanics) fundamentally alter the gameplay experience and change the optimal path towards getting certain achievements.

The descriptions on this page are taken from the in-game achievement list; the descriptions on Steam (or other platforms) may be different.
The Starting conditions column may denotes the requirements for the starting characters, preventing the use of certain tricks or custom rulers.
The DI column indicates the achievement's difficulty level as assigned by Paradox. It should be noted that this difficulty may not accord with player experience. For example, Saga in Stone is classified as Easy despite being generally considered one of the most challenging and time-consuming achievements without use of exploits; conversely, Carolingian Consolidation is relatively straightforward from the 1066 start.
The difficulty levels are:VE = Very Easy,E = Easy,M = Medium,H = Hard,VH = Very Hard.

Starting as Raja Indra Warmandewa of Kutai in 867, immediately submit to Raja Aditya of Tanjungpura as a tributary and take the decision (which should be immediately available) to adopt Mandala government. To unlock the Complex Great Project (found in the Projects tab of the Decisions menu), you need a Legitimacy of 1, Level of Devotion of 2, and at least one Tributary (which you can easily get from wars or the Embrace as Tributary interaction).
From here, all you need to do is gain Gold, Piety, and Legitimacy, the latter two coming via Mandala Rituals on the map and the Pilgrimage activity, and the former coming from the buildings you have right off the bat (you don't need much). Once you have enough, fund the Great Project and wait 5 years for it to be completed to get the achievement.

The requirements for the Rite of Worthiness will change depending on your Mandala Aspect. Destruction is the fastest, but will require you to have a high martial spouse set to Chivalry.

Shōgun icon
Shōgun
Become Shōgun, holding the Empire of Japan as Minamoto no Yoritomo in 1178
Yes Playing as Minamoto no Yoritomo
Took the Formalize Shogunate decision

Upon game start, immediately leave your current Bloc and found a new one by inviting independent (i.e, not part of a Bloc) fellow vassals to it. Start hunts and other prestige-gaining activities, and take the Assert Regional Dominion decision when available.
Additionally, ensure that all of the House Heads in your Bloc follow the same Aspiration as you, accomplished via the "Demand Aspiration Change" interaction. This will cause your Bloc Cohesion to rapidly rise, and a Cohesion of 75 is required for the next step of the achievement.

Once your Bloc has greater military strength than the current Daijo-daijin, start an "Install Ruling Regent" faction and press your demands. Win the war if necessary, and relish in your new title as Daijo-daijin. You now have to meet a few conditions, including having both Legitimacy and Prestige at level 4, and having the third level of crown authority (Powerful Ministries) - Japan starts at level 2. These are relatively easy to accomplish with activities, and once met, you can take the Formalize Shogunate decision. Must be accomplished within the lifetime of Minamoto no Yorimoto.

Upper-upper
Reach the highest level of Merit
 Merit Rank 1	You need to play as a vassal with  Celestial government. Pick or create a character with high Learning. Start by completing the available Examinations and using the Study scheme. Join every Examination you see, prioritizing Capital Examinations if one is happening, and always pick options that give Merit. Once appointed to a governorship, either recruit Men-at-Arms (if a Military governor) or build buildings and increase your tax income (if any other type of governor) to rapidly increase your passive Merit gain. Sword of Japan icon
Sword of Japan
Starting as the Chrysanthemum Throne, restore direct imperial rule
 The Chrysanthemum Throne	 Owns the Empire of Japan title
Taken the Restore Direct Imperial Rule decision

There are two ways to go about this, which differ based on the starting date, your patience, and whether you'd like to enjoy some historical flavor. Do not start in 1178, as the realm will already be Feudal (Soryo) and the achievement will be quite difficult.
Starting in 867 as Korehito, use political schemes and influence to get yourself to the front of the line of succession as soon as possible. The reigning Kampaku is old and will die within a few years of game start. Once elected to the regency, choose to abdicate, passing the ceremonial throne to your heir whilst keeping control over the Empire itself (this is a historical method of imperial rule that Japan actually used for a while).

You now want to ensure your heir is first in line for the Empire, whilst biding your time as you await the Early Medieval era and the Royal Prerogative innovation. Once you have this innovation and have subsequently died and started playing as your heir (who must now hold both the Empire and the Chrysanthemum Throne--DO NOT abdicate), you will be able to pass the third level of crown authority and take the decision.

Starting as Chikahito in 1066, you can either use politics to be elected to the throne as above (difficult as there are less Japanese rulers in 1066 with access to influence to help you), or use the Depose scheme (Intrigue lifestyle focus will help with this) to overthrow the ruling Kampaku. Either way, once on the throne you should already have access to the decision, if not, use activities to boost prestige to enact the requisite laws and take the decision. If starting in this date, do not abdicate upon ascending the throne - you already have Royal Prerogative unlocked and there is no need to abdicate to ensure your line can stay on the throne.
