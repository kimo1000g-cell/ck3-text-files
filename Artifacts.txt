Artifacts
Jump to navigationJump to search
This article has been verified for the current PC version (1.19) of the game.


Inventory artifacts
Artifacts are objects unique in the world that give various bonuses to the character using them and are always inherited by the primary heir. Artifacts are divided into two categories: inventory artifacts and court artifacts.

Inventory artifacts are worn by a character via their inventory. Each character can wear up to 9 artifacts: a crown, a journal, a weapon, an armor, a regalia and 4 trinkets.
Court artifacts are displayed in a character's royal court. Each court can display up to 15 artifacts: 3 large wall ornaments, 3 small wall ornaments, 2 pieces of furniture, 2 book items, 4 pedestal items, and a throne.
Artifacts may be given as gifts to other characters. This will grant +20  Opinion if the receiving character can use the artifact and +5  Opinion if it cannot.

Inventory artifacts that are not worn as well as court artifacts can be damaged or lost if the realm capital is besieged. If an artifact is lost during a siege its previous owner will gain a claim on it. All house heads will have a claim on the Dynasty Banner of their dynasty. Having a claim allows a character to demand the return of their artifact although accepting requires sufficiently superior military strength. More importantly, artifact claims allow the claimant to use the Artifact Claim War casus belli or challenge the artifact owner to a duel for the artifact. Rulers who have the  Accomplished Forger perk can also buy claims on artifacts, with the cost depending on the rarity of the artifact.

DLC Coronations.png	Available only with the Coronations DLC enabled.
Rulers with the Uncrowned.png Uncrowned law cannot use Artifact regalia.png Regalia artifacts if their religion is Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, Hausa, Finno-Ugric or Slavic and cannot use Artifact crown.png Crown artifacts if their religion is any other. But owning such an artifact is necessary to increase Activity coronation.png Coronation activity options.

Rarity
All artifacts have a Rarity, which determines the artifact's Durability, cost to repair and how much  Gold is obtained if the artifact is manually destroyed.

All artifacts have a Durability, which represents the integrity of the artifact. When it reaches zero, the artifact is destroyed. If a non-historical artifact is inherited by someone who is not a close family member, it will also lose Durability. All artifacts will lose 1 Durability each year except for artifacts displayed in the royal court. Durability loss can be reduced by employing an Antiquarian court position.png Antiquarian, which also enables artifacts to be reforged. Reforging an artifact costs  Gold, but will replenish its Durability. When reforging an artifact, its owner will always have the option to turn it into a Pedestal Item artifact, if it's not one already. Doing so will also change an artifact's effects. As adventurers cannot employ an Antiquarian court position.png Antiquarian, they cannot reforge an artifact, but can still repair one.

The cost of repairing an artifact is influenced by the remaining durability. Base repair cost also depends on an internal not visible ingame value calculation, some famed artifacts will count as illustrious for repairs for example. Base repair costs in the table below are a guideline.

AI rulers will never go to war over a Common artifact.

Creating artifacts
For rulers the main way of obtaining artifacts is via the Commission Artifact decision, which allows them to choose what type of artifact they want created and costs 100  Gold. While an artifact is created various events can take place, allowing the ruler to pay further to produce a higher rarity artifact. Pedestal Item artifacts cannot be created this way.

For adventurers the main way of obtaining artifacts is by visiting City holdings and buying them with  Gold. A Golden Coat of the Swan armor can be obtained via the Knight of the Swan decision.

The Commission Epic decision will create a Book artifact if the character has a royal court and a Journal artifact if the character does not have a royal court.

If a character wins a game of chess there is a 5% chance to gain a trinket artifact called Lucky Go Board if the character is in Eastern Asia and Lucky Chess Board if the character is somewhere else. The chance is increased to 25% if the game was played for a county. When offered, the artifact can also be destroyed to lose 10  Stress or improved for a moderate amount of  Gold.

If the DLC Tours and Tournaments.png Tours and Tournaments DLC is installed, winning Grand Tournament contests will reward artifacts. Their rarity depends on the Prizes activity option. All contests can award trinket or pedestal item artifacts, but certain contests can also award weapon, armor or bow artifacts.

If the DLC All Under Heaven.png All Under Heaven DLC is installed, tributaries of China can obtain a Fine Seal of Investiture regalia by paying tribute while they have sufficient Imperial Grace.

If a character has a Royal Court artifacts can be obtained the following ways:

When a pet dies there will be the option to pay 10-20  Gold to gain a small wall ornament.
Book artifacts can be obtained via the University Visit activity.
If a character wins a battle against a Noble opponent there is a chance to gain a Warbanner large wall ornament. Alternatively the artifact can be destroyed to gain 75  Prestige.
If a character has the  Arbitrary,  Deceitful,  Callous,  Sadistic or  Vengeful trait executing a prisoner has a 25% chance to be offered a Skull pedestal item. If the prisoner was a Nemesis the option will always be given. If the ruler does not have an Executioner court position.png Executioner it will lose -30  Opinion with the Personal Physician or Antiquarian.
If the Royal Court Royal Court DLC is installed, all characters will have a House Banner large wall ornament. If their last  Kingdom or above title the character who took the last title will get the artifact but if they're not part of the same house only the  Court Grandeur bonus will be given.
If the DLC All Under Heaven.png All Under Heaven DLC is installed, all independent rulers with  Celestial,  Meritocratic and  Meritocratic Khanate government as well as the owner of the  Chrysanthemum Throne title will have an Imperial Seal pedestal item.
If the DLC All Under Heaven.png All Under Heaven DLC is installed, tributaries of rulers with  Mandala government unlock the Create Bunga Mas decision, which can create a pedestal item.
If the DLC Friends & Foes.png Friends and Foes DLC is installed, when a nemesis dies characters with the  Callous,  Sadistic or  Vengeful trait can gain a Skull Goblet pedestal item. The option will not be available if the nemesis' faith has the Sky Burials funerary tradition.
Hunt trophies
Successful  Hunt activities will grant trinket artifacts. If the character has a Royal Court and the activity variant was not Hunt type falconry icon.png Falconry, the artifacts can also be wall ornaments. These artifacts will always give monthly  Prestige and depending on their rarity will also have additional effects.

Foxes, gazelles, hares, roes and non-exotic birds will always be Common
Hyenas, lynxes, wolves and exotic birds have a 97% chance to be Common and a 3% chance to be Masterwork
Antelopes, aurochs, bisons, reindeers and stags have a 50% chance to be Common and a 50% chance to be Masterwork
Bears, leopards, lions and tigers have an 85% chance to be Masterwork and a 15% chance to be Famed
Boars, elks and harts have a 33% chance to be Masterwork and a 67% chance to be Famed
If the activity variant was Hunt type legendary icon.png Legendary then the rarity will be doubled
A few rare events during the activity can also increase the rarity
Inspirations
Royal CourtRoads to Power	Available only with the Royal Court DLC or the Roads to Power DLC enabled.
Landless characters occasionally gain an Inspiration, allowing them to work on creating an artifact. Characters older than 13 can sponsor the inspirations with  Gold, with the cost depending on how skilled the inspired character is. Once an inspired character is financed they will start working on the artifact. The artifact's effects will depend on the inspired character's traits. The artifact rarity is decided by an average of the relevant skills plus modifiers from some cultures, buildings or events. There are two ways for characters to gain inspirations:

Inspired characters can randomly appear in royal courts. The greater a court's Grandeur the higher the chance that an inspired character will appear.
Followers of adventurers who have the Inspiration is Everywhere lifestyle perk can randomly gain inspirations.
There are multiple types of Inspiration, which determine what type of artifact will be created. If an inspiration can create more than one type of artifact there's a small chance the sponsor will be allowed to choose what sort of artifact they want created.

Icons
Roads to Power	Available only with the Roads to Power DLC enabled.
Icons are artifacts that can be created via the Commission Icon decision. There are three artifact options to choose from when taking the decision:

All characters can choose to create a Trinket artifact. For landed rulers the artifact will have Masterwork rarity and provide +0.3 or +0.4 monthly  Piety. For landless rulers there is a small chance the artifact will have Common rarity and provide +0.1 or +0.2 monthly  Piety.

Rulers with a royal court an choose to create a Large Wall Ornament or Small Wall Ornament artifact. The artifact will have two of the following bonuses:

 Monthly piety
 Temple holding and building construction cost reduction
 Zealot vassal tax contribution
 Zealot vassal opinion
 Clergy opinion

Travel itineraries
DLC Wandering Nobles.png	Available only with the Wandering Nobles DLC enabled.
Characters who have the Charts & Itineraries lifestyle perk can take the Create Travel Itinerary decision to create a journal artifact. When the decision is taken the character will have to choose what point of interest to write about. There will be up to 4 options depending on whether their type of point of interest has been visited. The bonuses provided scale with the amount of experience the  Traveler trait has.

Choosing to write about points of interest within the domain will bring the option to focus on resources or defense.
Focusing on resources will give Monthly Development in terrain matching one of the baronies with the point of interest.
Focusing on defense will give either Controlled Territory Defender Advantage or Men-at-Arms Maintenance Reduction.
Choosing to write about a point of interest that is a vassal capital will bring the option to focus on control, aid or secrets.
Focusing on control will give Dread Gain.
Focusing on aid will give either Powerful Vassal Councilor Tax Contribution or Prestige per Powerful Vassal on Council.
Focusing on secrets will give either Initial Hostile Scheme Success Chance or Scheme Secrecy.
Choosing to write about a point of interest that contains a special building will bring the option to focus on the travel or the destination.
Focusing on the travel will give Travel Speed and Travel Safety.
Focusing on the destination will give Monthly Experience to the lifestyle that received experience when the barony was visited. If the point of interest was a holy site it will instead give Monthly Piety. If the point of interest was a natural feature it will instead give Hunter Trait Experience Gain.
Choosing to write about a point of interest that is a neighbor capital will bring the option to focus on the realm or the people. Characters with the  Eccentric trait can also choose to write about a mythical creature.
Focusing on realm will give Monthly Prestige if the character does not have a royal court and Court Grandeur if the character has a royal court.
Focusing on people will give either Independent Ruler Opinion or Diplomacy.
Focusing on a mythical creature will give either Renown or Dynasty Opinion.
5 days later you will have to choose which aspect of the journey to write about.

You will always have the option to write about the terrain of the point of interest. The artifact will have the following bonus:
65% chance to give +5  Advantage in that terrain
35% chance to give +8  Advantage in that terrain
If the point of interest county had below 50  Control or was at war you will have the option to write about the danger. The artifact will have the following bonus:
25% chance to give +3  Defender Advantage in controlled territory
25% chance to give +2%  Enemy Fatal Casualties
50% chance to give +5  Travel Speed
If the point of interest was in the region was Northern Europe, Steppe or Tibet you will have the option to write about the winters. The artifact will add +10%  Movement Speed and +2  Advantage in baronies affected by winter.
If the point of interest was in the region of Africa, Burma, India or Middle East you will have the option to write about the heat. The artifact will add -23  Danger in Desert Mountains, Desert, Drylands and Jungle terrain.
If the point of interest was in the region of Burma or India you will have the option to write about the rains. The artifact will add -23  Danger in Desert Mountains, Jungle and Wetlands terrain.
Paizas
Khans of the Steppe	Available only with the Khans of the Steppe DLC enabled.
Paizas are trinket artifacts that are unlocked by the Establish Paiza System decision. The Gurkhan will receive a Paiza and can give additional ones to subjects who have  Nomadic government at the cost of 25  Gold and 350  Prestige. Rulers who do not have a Paiza and have  Nomadic government can also ask the Gurkhan to give them one. Rulers who receive a Paiza will receive 20 Legitimacy if  Count, 100 Legitimacy if  Duke, 200 Legitimacy if  King and 300 Legitimacy if  Emperor. The quality of a Paiza depends on the Gurkhan's title rank and Dominance law.

A Paiza will include the name of the Gurkhan who issued it. While they remain the Gurkhan, the Paiza will give +5  Advantage and -50  Danger in counties with  Steppe terrain. Characters other than the Gurkhan will gain access to the Abuse Gurkhan's Authority decision and can use the Leverage Khan Authority interaction on all characters with  Nomadic government. Paizas can be taken by other rulers but only characters who have  Nomadic government can use the decision and interaction.

If the character who issued a Paiza is no longer the Gurkhan, all Paizas lose all effects and will instead only give +0.05  Prestige per month. The new Gurkhan must take the Establish Paiza System decision before they can grant new Paizas.
