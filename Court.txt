Jump to navigationJump to search
This article has been verified for the current PC version (1.19) of the game.


Court positions
Each noble character has a court, which consists of the unlanded characters at a character's court. A court may contain family members, knights, lovers, and courtiers or followers. Rulers may also have hostages and guests.

Courtiers and followers
Courtiers and followers are unlanded characters living at a character's court: they are called courtiers if the liege is a ruler and adventurers if the liege is an adventurer. They can be appointed as councilors, commanders, knights or vassals. If a courtier does not have a court position or minor title they have a 2% chance to leave each month. The chance is decreased by −0.1% for each 10 points of  opinion above 0 and increased by +0.1% for each 10 points of  opinion below 0. In addition the chance to leave can be modified by the following:

Condition	Chance to leave
Child	−3%
Any claim	−1%
 Pregnant	−1%
Married to the liege	−0.5%
 Low health	−0.3%
Culture has the Cultural Primacy tradition	−0.3%
Culture has the Isolationist tradition	−0.3%
Liege or another courtier is a friend	−0.2%
Liege or another courtier is a lover	−0.2%
Liege or another courtier is a close family member	−0.2%
 Bastard	+0.3%
 Rival	+0.5%
Culture has the Audacious Cadets tradition	+0.5%
Culture has the Caravaneers tradition	+0.5%
Culture has the Swords for Hire tradition	+0.5%
Characters can ask another character's adult courtiers or followers to join them. Acceptance depends on the difference in  Opinion and Icon amenity lodgings.png Lodgings court amenities between both rulers, relation with both rulers, and whether the courtier has a council or court position or is a knight. The inviting ruler can spend 25  Gold to increase acceptance and can use a Hook to force it.

Courtiers and followers who aren't close family can be dismissed but this costs 75  Prestige and 20  Opinion with the courtier for 20 years.

Hostages
Hostages are landless children that are a Close Family member of another ruler who sends them to another ruler's court as an assurance of peace or submission. Hostages can be offered, demanded or exchanged both to prevent a war or to help end it. A character having a hostage will be called its Warden and will gain the following rewards:

 +0.1 to +1 Monthly Prestige depending on difference in title rank
 +0.1 to +1 Monthly Piety depending on difference in title rank if the hostage's faith is considered Hostile or Evil
 +0.25 to +0.5 Monthly Renown depending on the Level of Splendor of the hostage's dynasty if they are part of a different dynasty
When the hostage reaches the age of 16 there is a chance to gain a Hook-perpetual.png Perpetual Hook on them
If the hostage's liege declares war it will bring the option to kill the hostage and gain 10  Dread or spare it and gain 10  Opinion with the hostage
If a ruler declares war upon a Warden who has someone from their family as hostage it will lose a level of fame, 250  Prestige and 25  Opinion with all family members and suffer the following penalties for 5 years:

 -25 Opinion
 -50% Siege Phase Time
 -15% Army Damage
 -15% Army Toughness
Vassals with  Celestial,  Meritocratic and  Meritocratic Khanate government cannot demand or offer hostages to each other or their liege.

Guests
Guests are unlanded characters that stay at a ruler's court for three years and then have a 5% chance to leave each month. During this time they will interact with other members of the court and may take part in random events. They can be convinced to stay permanently in the realm by being given a seat on the council, a court position or a landed or minor title, or by using the seduction scheme. Guests can also be directly recruited by paying a variable amount of  Gold - the base amount is 15 gold and increases if the guest has claims, useful traits, high skills, or come from a dynasty with a high level of splendor. Guests are more likely to visit a court if they have a definite reason, such as the ruler being strong enough to press their claims, or the ruler or one of its adult children being unmarried. The fewer courtiers a character has the more guests it will receive.

If a guest with claims is about to leave, the liege will be given the option to convince the guest to stay for one year.

Court positions
Court positions are specialized roles that can be granted to courtiers and direct vassals and grant increasingly powerful benefits but for rulers cost a monthly salary. Characters who are imprisoned cannot be given court positions with the exception of Executioner. A ruler must pay 200  Prestige to fire someone with a court position while an adventurer must pay 25  Prestige. If a character's court position is revoked the character will gain a decaying −30  Opinion with the liege, which is removed if the character is given another court position. Court positions can be automated to either suggest the best candidate or automatically assign it.

Common and ruler court positions can perform 1-3 additional tasks, which grant it additional bonuses but also increase the salary by +0.06 per primary title rank.

Each court position has a salary in  Gold and possibly  Prestige, which is affected by the following:

−75% if government is  Tribal or  Wanua
−20% if culture has the Esteemed Hospitality tradition
+10% if realm size is 30-59
+20% if realm size is 60-89
+40% if realm size is 90-199
+60% if realm size is 120+
+25% if culture is is the Early Medieval era
+50% if culture is is the High Medieval era
+75% if culture is is the Late Medieval era
+25% if  Emperor
Roads to PowerDLC All Under Heaven.png	Available only with the Roads to Power DLC or the All Under Heaven DLC enabled.
If the ruler's government uses the  Treasury resource the salary will be paid with it instead of  Gold.

DLC All Under Heaven.png	Available only with the All Under Heaven DLC enabled.
The owner of the  Chrysanthemum Throne title cannot be given court positions unless they become landed.

Aptitude
The bonuses of a court position depend on the courtier or vassal's aptitude. Aptitude goes through 5 levels, each level requiring 20 points of aptitude:

Aptitude level	Minimum points
Terrible	0
Poor	20
Average	40
Good	60
Excellent	80
+20 Aptitude for Family members of the same culture (Culture has the Family Business tradition).

−20 Aptitude for Vassals.

−75 Aptitude for  Infirm characters (−50 for the Caravan master court position.png Caravan Master, none for the Court gardener court position.png Court Gardener).

Recruit for a Court Position
Aside from manually assigning courtiers or guests to a court position, it is possible to recruit new characters both via a button in the Court tab and via a decision. This will bring an event where the ruler can choose between a character with above average Aptitude and one with below average Aptitude. Both options have an upfront  Gold cost that scales with the character's Aptitude and the ruler's monthly income. Only the following court positions can be selected:  Personal Physician,  Caravan Master,  Court Gardener,  Chief Eunuch,  Wet Nurse, Bookmaker court position.png Bookmaker, Charioteer court position.png Champion Charioteer, Foreign emissary court position.png Foreign Emissary, Siege engineer court position.png Siege Engineer,  Stargazer, Khlon glan court position.png Khlon Glan and Court brahmin court position.png Court Brahmin. Rulers who have at least 15  Learning or the  Scholar,  Theologian or  Whole of Body trait will always have the option to recruit a  Personal Physician with Excellent aptitude.
