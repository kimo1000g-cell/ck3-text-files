This article has been verified for the current PC version (1.19) of the game.


The subjects tab
A subject is a ruler who submits to a more powerful ruler, providing them with resources in exchange for not being attacked and, in some cases, military protection from other threats. There are three kinds of subjects: vassals, tributaries, and lessees. Tributaries may have their own subjects, but vassals can only have their own vassals and lessees, and if a ruler is vassalized all of their tributaries become independent. Vassals can be turned into tributaries at any point, but turning a tributary into a vassal requires the tributary to agree.

Subject tithes will be reduced if a ruler does not own the titles that the vassal's primary title is de jure part of. Dukes whose liege holds the de jure empire title are exempt from the penalty.

There is no reduction if the ruler owns the title 1 rank above the subject (i.e. the  Kingdom title a  Duchy vassal is de jure part of)
There is a 25% reduction if the ruler only owns a title 2 ranks above the subject (i.e. the  Kingdom title a  Count vassal is de jure part of)
There is a 50% reduction if the ruler does not own any title that the vassal's primary title is de jure part of
If the liege or suzerain's government uses  Treasury the subject's tax income will be added as  Treasury instead of  Gold.

Vassals
A vassal is a subject who surrenders its sovereignty to a ruler with a higher title rank, called their liege. A vassal will join all its liege's wars (unless they're fighting against them in a civil war), but is not obligated to actively participate in the wars and will generally not do so. A liege will join a vassal's defensive wars, unless they're being attacked by a fellow vassal (though this can be prohibited by the Crown Authority law). A vassal will provide their liege with  Taxes and  Levies. The amount of  taxes and  Levies granted by a vassal to their liege depends largely on their government:

 Administrative,  Celestial,  Meritocratic,  Meritocratic Khanate and  Ritsuryō vassals provide taxes and levies based on their governor efficiency.
 Feudal and  Sōryō vassals provide taxes and levies based on their vassal contracts.
 Tribal and  Wanua vassals provide taxes and levies based on the liege's level of fame, with minimum amounts determined by tribal authority. For details, see Fame.
 Clan vassals provide taxes and levies based on their opinion towards their liege, with minimum amounts determined by crown authority. If the liege has the same government the vassal will have to be assigned to a Tax Jurisdiction to provide taxes and levies.
 Nomadic vassals provide taxes and herd based on their vassal contracts.
Theocracy government.png Theocracy vassals provide taxes and levies based on the liege's level of devotion.
Republic government.png Republic vassals always provide 20% taxes and 10% levies to their liege.
If a character is granted a title all lower-ranked vassals whose realms are located entirely within the de jure realm of the granted title will be transferred.

Vassal borders and flags do not appear on the map unless any of the vassals is selected, with the exception of the player's own vassals.

Vassal limit
Every ruler has a Vassal Limit. Each vassal above the Vassal Limit reduces the  Taxes and  Levies of all vassals by -5%, up to a maximum penalty of -95%. Baron vassals do not count towards the Vassal Limit. For most governments the vassal limit is decided by the title rank but there are a few exceptions:

For  Administrative rulers the Vassal Limit is decided by the title rank and the level of authority law
For  Nomadic rulers the Vassal Limit is decided by the Dominance law
For  Mandala rulers the Vassal Limit is decided by the Level of Devotion and the level of their Capital Temple Complex building
For  Wanua rulers the Vassal Limit is increased by building tribe buildings
If the Conquerors: Bonuses game rule has been set to Extreme AI conquerors will have a vassal limit of 500 regardless of government
Vassal directives
Vassals can be given a directive either by interacting with them or from the Vassals tab. Directives can be used to influence what kind of buildings a vassal will construct in its sub-realm or make them convert the sub-realm's faith or culture. Vassals can only be given a directive if one of the following requirements are met and will stop following the directive if none of the requirements are met.

Powerful vassals
In each realm, vassals with the income of  Gold and  Levies are designated as powerful vassals, who expect to be part of the liege's council and will have -40  opinion of their liege if they're not. The number of powerful vassals a liege will have depends on the rank of their primary title:

 Counts will have 3
 Dukes will have 4
 Kings and above will have 5
In elective succession types, powerful vassals have more votes than regular vassals. They're also much harder to use the sway scheme on. In order for the succession law of a realm to be changed, all powerful vassals must either have positive  opinion of the liege, be Terrified.png terrified or be imprisoned.

The Vassal Stance determines which of their liege's actions they will like and/or dislike, as well as which of their liege's heirs they prefer, according to the gender succession law of the realm. If the heir preferred by the vassal is the Primary Heir of their liege, they will gain a temporary +30  Opinion upon succession. If the vassal does not prefer the Primary Heir, they will instead gain a temporary -20  Opinion upon succession. There are 7 Vassal Stances:

Vassal contracts
All  Feudal,  Clan,  Nomadic and  Sōryō vassals have a vassal contract with their liege, which determines their obligations as well as whether they have special rights. The contract can only be changed once per lifetime of either liege or vassal and this limit is shared between the liege and the vassal. Up to 4 things can be changed at once in a vassal contract.

Rights
Rights grant a  vassal permissions that vassals do not have by default. When changing the contract, a score is calculated for how desirable the previous and new setup of the contract are to the vassal, including all rights and obligations. For every point the score of the new total contract is lower than it was, the liege suffers 20 tyranny. Rights can be added in favor of the subject or a  weak hook can be used to compensate for 1 score point when increasing an obligation in order to avoid tyranny. Up to 4 changes can be proposed at once for balanced changes, but if tyranny is already incurred you may then not add another change favoring the liege.

The liege can also use a hook on the vassal to avoid tyranny.

Factions
Factions are organized groups that are united against their liege for a common purpose. As long as their issue isn't resolved, they will keep growing until they will be large enough to deliver an ultimatum to the liege. Faction members unlock an interaction with all characters to force them to join the faction for 10 years if they have a  Strong Hook on them or 500  Influence.

All factions have two important values: military power and discontent.

Military Power is the ratio between the combined military strength of all faction members and the military strength of the liege. When the ratio is above 80% the Faction will gain discontent and when the ratio is below 80%, the faction will lose discontent.
Discontent is a measure of how close the faction is to sending their ultimatum. How fast Discontent increases or falls depends on how far above or below the Military Power threshold the faction is.
A faction will deliver its ultimatum shortly after discontent reaches 100%. It can also send the ultimatum early if the liege unjustly imprisons someone. Player-led factions can send the ultimatum at any time. If the ultimatum is accepted, the liege will lose -20  Dread and  Legitimacy. If it is refused, it will start a civil war.

Vassals cannot create or join a faction against a liege if they are landless, underage, Ceremonial Monarch, the liege has a  Strong Hook, alliance or truce with them or is in the same bloc as them, or they have at least 80  Opinion of the liege. They also cannot join a faction if they are terrified unless discontent is rising. If one of these requirements is met after a character joins a faction, the character will leave the faction. They can however be forced into a faction by the use of a Hook. AI vassals cannot start or join a faction against their liege if they are friends or lovers but player vassals can. Vassals with a bureaucratic government can only create factions if their title rank is  King and vassals with  Ritsuryō government can only create factions if they are house head and their house is leading a bloc with at least 50 Cohesion.

Civil wars
If a faction's ultimatum is refused, it will start a Civil War. During a Civil War, faction members stop providing taxes and levies to the liege and their liege loses access to certain powers such as imprisonment. All faction members will turn hostile to both the liege and all vassals that did not join the faction, though they will focus on fighting the liege. If all faction members are gone during a civil war the war will end in white peace if the liege had negative warscore and in victory for the liege if they had positive warscore.

If the war ends in white peace, the liege will gain an imprisonment reason against all faction members.
If the war is won by the faction, they will enforce the faction's ultimatum.
If the war is won by the liege, all faction members are imprisoned and the liege gains +20  Dread. If the faction was caused by  popular opinion, each rebelling county will gain +20  popular opinion for 10 years. The liege will also have cause to revoke a title from each faction member (allowing the liege to revoke a title without gaining tyranny).
Vassal factions
Vassal factions can only be created manually by vassals. They all require sufficient military power to gain Discontent and their military power is calculated by comparing their members' armies with the armies of the liege and every vassal who is not a member of the faction. AI vassals cannot form these factions during the first 10 years if the liege is an  Emperor, first 5 years if the liege is a  King, or first 1 year if the liege is a  Duke or below. How likely a faction is to be created or joined depends on the Realm Stability game rule.

Popular factions
Popular factions cannot be created or joined by vassals and are created automatically if counties in the  Domain have negative Popular Opinion. How negative Popular Opinion has to be depends on the Realm Stability game rule. Popular factions' military power is calculated by comparing the  Levies produced by the member counties with the  Levies produced by the owner's domain. Popular factions gain discontent twice faster if the Borders Wars Policy law is set to Conditional Join or the liege is part of the Dynastic Cycle situation and the era is Dynastic cycle phase instability.png Tension. If a popular faction captures a character during a siege it will kill them instead of imprisoning them. Civil wars against popular factions will end if their leader dies.

Tributaries
A tributary is the most independent subject type, paying tribute in exchange for a truce with a ruler with higher or equal title rank, called their suzerain. Rulers with  Nomadic,  Celestial and  Mandala government can have tributaries regardless of authority law and can ask their neighbors to become tributaries. Other governments can only have tributaries after increasing their authority law to level 2 and can only gain them through war. Independent rulers can always offer to become tributaries to rulers that can have them. Tributary borders are visible on the map.

Becoming a tributary creates a truce. After the truce expires the tributary can become independent at the cost of 75  Prestige and 100  Opinion with the former suzerain.

Independent rulers can only become tributaries of a  Hegemony by paying tribute once first, and they will be expected to further pay tribute on a regular basis.

Every tributary except Herder has a contract with their suzerain, which can only be changed once per character and determines their obligations as well as whether they have special rights. What tribute options are available in the contract depends on the tributary type. Herder tributaries do not have a contract and give 20% of their  Herd to their suzerain instead.

For all tributaries except those of China there are also two possible options that may be added to their contract:

Suzerain Guarantee: Guaranteed allows the tributary to call the suzerain into their defensive wars and increase the tributary's  Opinion by +25.
Tributary War Support: Forced brings the tributary into the suzerain's wars and reduces their  Opinion by -25 and is only available if the tributary is Obedient or a Blood Brother.
Tributaries have a yearly chance to stop paying tribute depending on their military strength relative to that of their suzerain. Should that happen, the suzerain will have the option to either let them become independent or go to war.

Pay tribute
DLC All Under Heaven.png	Available only with the All Under Heaven DLC enabled.
Tributaries can travel to their suzerain directly to pay tribute via the Pay Tribute decision. The amount of tribute paid depends on culture era and whether the suzerain is a  Rival or Nemesis.png Nemesis. The following tribute options are available:

 Gold is available to every tributary that does not have Treasury.
 Treasury is available to every tributary uses the resource.
 Herd is available to Nomadic tributaries.
 Artifact is available to tributaries that have an unequipped artifact.
 Concubine is available if the suzerain has the Concubines tenet or tradition and can take additional concubines. Unlike other forms of tribute this one requires the suzerain to accept the concubine.
 Eunuch is available if the suzerain's culture has the Court Eunuchs, Byzantine Traditions, Palace Politics or Court Machinations tradition and requires the tributary to have a courtier with the trait.
Once the tribute is selected the character will start traveling to the suzerain's capital. Once there the tributary will receive one of the following rewards:

100  Legitimacy.
An artifact of random quality. Quality cannot be Illustrious unless the tributary and the suzerain are best friends.
A county modifier in the realm capital that lasts for 10 years and gives +10%  Tax and +20%  Development.
An innovation. This is only available if the characters have different cultures and the suzerain's culture has an innovation the tributary's culture doesn't.
A courtier of the suzerain's faith with the  Monk trait. This is only available if the suzerain's government is  Mandala, the tributary has an unreformed faith and the characters do not follow the same faith.
 Treasury that scales with amount of tribute paid and the suzerain's opinion. This is only available if the suzerain is Hegemon of China and will be converted into  Gold if the tributary does not use  Treasury.
The tributary can try a random skill challenge to choose the gift but if the challenge is failed it will gain 5  Stress.

Hegemons can ask tributaries to pay tribute and can ask what kind. The tributary must accept and if is refuses it will lose 50  Legitimacy.

Hegemonic tributaries
Independent rulers unlock the Pay Tribute decision on  Hegemons too and using it is necessary to become a tributary. Tributaries of hegemonies track a value called Subject Standing, or Imperial Grace if the suzerain is China, which ranges from 0 to 100 and is visible when reviewing the contract. Unlike other subject contracts, their contracts can be changed every 5 years. Tributaries can only request a change in their contract if they have at least 20 Subject Standing and decreasing an obligation without increasing the other costs 10 Subject Standing for each level. New tributaries, as well as the tributaries present at start of the game, will have 25 Subject Standing. Every time the tributary pays tribute they will receive between 5 and 15 Subject Standing if they did not attempt or failed the skill challenge and between 20 and 30 Subject Standing if they succeeded the skill challenge. AI hegemons are more likely to demand that tributaries pay their tribute if their Subject Standing is low. Subject Standing decreases by 1 each year and if the suzerain is China this decrease is doubled if the Dynastic Cycle era is Dynastic cycle phase expansion.png Expansion and tripled if it is Dynastic cycle phase instability.png Tension or Dynastic cycle phase conquest.png Conquest.

Tributaries of China
DLC All Under Heaven.png	Available only with the All Under Heaven DLC enabled.
Tributaries of China can be offered Seals of Investiture artifacts if their Imperial Grace is sufficient when they pay tribute. They need 70 if  Count, 50 if  Duke and 30 if  King and above. Accepting the artifact costs 500  Treasury or  Gold and gives +50  Legitimacy. Refusing the artifact costs 50  Legitimacy and 50  Opinion with the Hegemon of China. Having a Seal of Investiture unlocks the following contract privileges:

Family Access increases the tributary's Marriage Acceptance with members of the suzerain's dynasty and removes the Marriage Acceptance penalties from having different cultures
Market Access gives the tributary up to +50%  Development Growth in its realm capital
Palace Access makes the tributary an active participant in the Dynastic Cycle situation and gives them  Movement Power
Lessees
Lessees are clergy subjects who own baronies. Their baronies are still considered part of its holder's realm, but provides levies and taxes to its lessee instead, and does not count towards the domain limit of either of them. The game considers a character with only leased titles to be unlanded. A lessee can also be a vassal if they have a County or higher title.

When founding a holy order, a castle or city must be leased to the order. Leases to holy orders can be revoked, although this costs 500  Piety, 40  Opinion with the holy order's grandmaster's, 20 opinion  Opinion with the head of faith of the holder, and will destroy the holy order entirely if its last lease is revoked.

Realm priests
When a ruler's religion has the  Theocratic clerical tradition, temple holdings are leased to the Bishop job.png Realm Priest councilor. The realm priest provides their liege with taxes and levies based on their opinion. The minimum is at 0 opinion, where no taxes or levies are provided, and the maximum is at 50 opinion, where 50% of the taxes and 100% of the levies are provided. Unlike leases to holy orders, there is no option to revoke leased temple holdings.

For vassals of the same religion, a vassal's income from their realm priest's church holdings are not directly taxed by their liege. The vassal's realm priest provides their liege's realm priest with 25% of their temples' taxes, and 15% of their temples' levies. This vassal realm priest contribution is provided by the liege realm priest to the liege at the same rate as directly leased holdings. Vassals of different religions are skipped in the hierarchy of lease payments. In addition, if the Faith also has a Spiritual Head of Faith, then the Realm Priest of the independent landed characters (i.e. the top lieges) pay that 15% taxes and the 25% levies to the said Head of Faith.

If you have multiple domain counties that have temple holdings, your Realm Priest would have multiple holdings under his/her management. In such case, it may be beneficial to have a Realm Priest that has high stewardship/martial to enjoy the benefit of extra taxes/levies. At 50+ opinion, each point of your Realm Priest's stewardship would give you 1% more taxes and each point of martial would give you 2% more levies, from all such temple holdings.
