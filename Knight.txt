Knight
Jump to navigationJump to search
This article has been verified for the current PC version (1.19) of the game.


The knights tab
Knights are characters that fight in an army. Each ruler can only have a small number of Knights. By default, any available characters will be appointed as knight based on its  Prowess skill, but this behavior can be changed in the Permissions menu. Diarchs cannot be appointed as knights.

If an army loses a Battle, each Knight has a 10% chance to be captured and imprisoned and 5% chance to be killed. The chances are increased to 55.3% and 27.6% respectively if the army is defeated in the Early Battle Phase.

Appointment
Each ruler can only have a small number of Knights. By default, Knights are appointed automatically based on their  Prowess skill, but characters can be manually forced or forbidden to become Knights.

To be appointed as Knights a character must fulfil the following conditions:

Is adult
Is not imprisoned
Is not diarch
Does not have the  Blind or  Incapable trait
Members of the clergy and characters with the  Devoted trait may only serve as knights if their faith has the  Recruitment Clerical Function doctrine, the Warmonger tenet, or the Unrelenting Faith tenet.
If Royal Court Royal Court is installed the ruler's culture's Martial Custom must allow it.
If Royal Court Royal Court is not installed the ruler's faith's View on Gender must allow it.
Combat performance
Knights represent a significant fraction of the total realm fighting force. Their strong side is extremely high damage per combat width, which helps to out-damage and even stackwipe larger enemy armies. They are also free in terms of monthly maintenance. Their weakness is low total toughness, requiring the use of Men-at-Arms or levies to absorb damage for them, and the potential to get wounded, killed or captured in combat.

Knights can be wounded, maimed (one-eyed, one-legged, disfigured, maimed, incapable), killed and captured. The chance of being wounded and killed decreases with higher levels of  Prowess or if their army outnumbers the enemy's or the game difficulty was reduced. Knights with Berserker traits may instantly kill other knights if they are lower than 80% of the Berserker knight's Prowess.

Knights combat stats are based on their  Prowess skill with each point of  Prowess grants 50  Damage and 10  Toughness, further modified by realm Knight Effectiveness.

To put these numbers into perspective, one levy soldier has 10  Damage and 10  Toughness, and so 1 knight with 10 prowess and base effectiveness deals as much damage as 50 levies. For an early game tribal realm it is not uncommon to field 7 knights with 15 average prowess and 120% effectiveness, which amounts to as much damage as 630 levies.

Accolades

An accolade
DLC Tours and Tournaments.png	Available only with the Tours and Tournaments DLC enabled.
Accolades are special named honors granted to knights by rulers at the cost of 100-200  Prestige, depending on primary title rank. Accolades can only be unlanded knights with at least 8  Prowess and have a +15  Opinion of their liege. Accolades are less likely to die in battle or be captured than knights.

When an accolade dies it is replaced by a courtier who meets the attribute requirements, or a newly-created character if no courtier meets the requirements. Each accolade has a Squire Aptitude, which determines the  Martial,  Prowess and traits of its successor and is increased by the following:

Rank
Accolades have a rank, which ranges from 1 to 6. Each time its rank increases it gains an attribute point, which can be used to either level up an existing attribute or gain an additional one, up to 3. Ranks are increased by gaining sufficient Glory, which can be obtained in the following ways:

Their army is victorious
Their liege wins a war against a ruler with higher title rank
They win combat against other knights
They attend activities, especially Grand tournaments
Rank	1	2	3	4	5	6
Glory	100	300	600	1000	1500	2100
Accolades lose 200 Glory upon succession if the previous accolade did not die in battle or duel but even if the rank is reduced existing accolade attributes are not downgraded or removed. There is no maximum limit to Glory.
