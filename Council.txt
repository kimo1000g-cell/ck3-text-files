Jump to navigationJump to search
This article has been verified for the current PC version (1.19) of the game.


A council
The council is a group of specialized vassals or courtier that help a landed character rule their realm. If a councilor is also a commander, it cannot lead troops while assigned to a task.

Each councilor can be assigned to one of a number of tasks. Current progress towards a councilor task is lost if the task is changed, except for Integrate Title and Increase Development/Control in County. Progress is not lost if the councilor dies. While a councilor is performing a task, one event related to it can trigger each year, although they are prohibited from occurring in the first month of assignment. Positive events can take place if the councilor's skill is above 8 and negative events can take place if the councilor's skill is below 15. Each task's effect scales linearly with the councilor's skill. In addition, all tasks are performed 20% more efficiently if the councilor is a  Friend of the liege, 30% more efficiently if the councilor is a  Best Friend of the liege, 20% less efficiently if the councilor is a  Rival of the liege, and 30% less efficiently if the councilor is a Nemesis.png Nemesis of the liege.

Characters cannot be appointed Councilors if they have the  Incapable trait.

Religious affairs councilor
There are two possible religious affair councilors, depending on the Clerical Tradition doctrine of the ruler's faith. Both of them use  Learning as their primary skill. Who appoints them and for how long depends on the faith's Clerical Appointment doctrine, and only characters who have the same faith as the liege and are allowed by the Clerical Gender doctrine can be appointed. A character with the  Excommunicated trait cannot be appointed to this council position if faith has the Communion tenet unless the liege has the same trait.

Religious councillor job.png Court Chaplain is the position available if the faith has the  Lay Clergy doctrine and has no special mechanics.
Bishop job.png Realm Priest is the position available if the faith has the  Theocratic doctrine. The court positions leases all Temple holdings in the liege's domain and receive a portion of the Taxes and Levies of every vassal's Realm Priest of the same faith. Realm Priests provide their liege with taxes and levies equal to their Opinion, ranging from 0 to 50. They provide all their Taxes and Levies regardless of Opinion if their liege has a Strong Hook on them.

Hegemony of China council
DLC All Under Heaven.png	Available only with the All Under Heaven DLC enabled.
As long as the Hegemony of China has  Celestial government it will have 8 additional council positions. Councilors receive a salary that is decided in the realm budget allocation and their domicile location will always be the liege's realm capital. The councilors receive the following bonuses in addition to the usual councilor bonuses:

Yes Additional interactions with fellow vassals
 A titular Empire title
 +1 Max Hostile, Personal and Political schemes
 +8 Monthly Influence
Favored minister court position.png Can be given the Favored Minister council position
Minister bureaucrat court position.png Unlock the Department Aide council position
 Can take the Request Bestowal of Honors decision
Yes Influence Career interaction has an additional option: Grant Examination Exemption. This option costs 600  Influence and automatically passes all examinations for the selected family member. If a minister uses the Grant Examination Exemption option more than once it will have to pay 2000  Merit for each use.
The councilors cannot have landed titles and will trigger a succession if they own any when they're appointed. They cannot have their claims used in casus belli and if an ongoing war used such a casus belli the war will end. They also cannot create Independence faction.png Independence factions.

When a councilor dies it is automatically replaced with another character who has a Merit Rank of 3 or above who has the highest score. The liege can manually replace councilors but if the new councilor's Merit Rank is below 3 the era will be pushed towards Dynastic cycle phase instability.png Tension. Score is based on the following:

+100 if movement leader
+100 if same culture as the liege and liege took the Promote Cultural Governors decision
+25 if movement is the favored movement
+20 if Elder of the current councilor
+10 if Disciple of the current councilor
+10 if age is above 40
+10 per level of influence
+1 per skill used by the council position
-5 if age is below 30
All bonuses are reduced by 75% if the character is older than the retirement age
All bonuses are reduced by 80% if the character is not favored by the gender law
The hegemon can use the following additional interactions with councilors:

Request Mentorship requires choosing a character at the court. The councilor will gain +30  Merit and the selected character will become the councilor's Disciple. The councilor can be asked to give the selected character  Merit, a commander trait the councilor has, or the  Confucian Education trait if the councilor has it.
Requisition Treasury asks to take half of the councilor's  Treasury, up to a maximum of 400 per councilor title rank. It costs 30  Influence and the cost is increased by 10 for each Merit Rank the councilor has. The councilor can refuse but refusing costs 60  Influence and the cost is increased by 30 for each level of  Merit that the councilor has lower than the liege.
Request Commander is only available with the Minister of War councilor and allows selecting a courtier of the councilor to add to the hegemon's court. The interaction costs 55  Treasury and the selected courtier must be adult, allowed to be a commander, and cannot be a close family member of the councilor.
