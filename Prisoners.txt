Prisoner
Jump to navigationJump to search
Please help with verifying or updating older sections of this article.
At least some were last verified for version 1.18.

This article is for the PC version of Crusader Kings 3 only.


Two imprisoned nobles
Prisoners are characters who are held at someone's court unwillingly. A character can obtain prisoners via the Abduction scheme, occupying a character's realm capital while at war or following battles if the enemy commander has low  Prowess. A ruler can attempt to imprison any character within their realm but this will incur tyranny, particularly if the prisoner is Noble, unless they are guilty of a Crime. An imprisoned character can be released, ransomed, tortured or executed.

If a prisoner is the third or closer in line to a ruler's primary title it will provide War Score in war against the said ruler but will be released at the end of any such war.

Imprisonment effects
Characters will be imprisoned in either the dungeon or under house arrest and can be freely moved between the two. By default prisoners are held in the dungeon if Lowborn and house arrest if Noble. While imprisoned they suffer the following penalties:

A prisoner will gain  Stress while in prison and will eventually die if they have too much stress.

Releasing a prisoner with no demands costs 10  Dread and gives 20  Opinion for 10 years with the prisoner. Releasing a character with no demands may give  Legitimacy if their title rank is high enough.

Ransom
A ruler can ransom imprisoned characters, exchanging  gold or a  weak hook for the prisoner's freedom. The Ransom interaction is used on the prisoner, not the recipient. The character who is sent the ransom is decided in the following order:

The prisoner themselves if they are landed
Their direct liege if prisoner is a courtier
The prisoner themselves, if they are unlanded
Prisoners and their direct liege may also offer to pay ransom to the jailer, with an additional option of using a hook on the jailer. Vassals and courtiers who have their ransom paid for by their liege gain +5  opinion for 1 year.

When paying with  gold, the ransom cost of a given prisoner depends on the ransom recipient's primary title rank as well as the prisoner's relation with the recipient. If a spouse is also the primary heir and/or close family, only the spouse price applies. If the ransom recipient is unable to afford the full price of the ransom, the price is reduced to as much gold as they have left. If the jailor has the Golden Obligations perk, all ransom costs are increased to approximately 6 times the jailor's tax income (minimum of 50, maximum of 300 gold). Characters who are unlanded and liegeless cost 100 gold to ransom if the jailer has the Golden Obligations perk (can be increased further) or if the character at least 100 gold to spare.

Punishment
Punishing a character will may grant  Dread depending on punishment and the prisoner's title rank

Execution
Executing an imprisoned character gives -20  Opinion with the victim's dynasty, -25  Opinion with the victim's liege, -30  Opinion with the victim's friends and lovers, -50  Opinion with the victim's family and spouses, and +2 Tyranny unless the prisoner is guilty of a very severe crime or follows a faith considered Hostile or Evil. The execution can be enhanced with the following options, which will add some additional effects to it:

Characters with the  Cannibal trait or whose faith has the Ritual Cannibalism tenet can choose to devour the prisoner, which grants -35  Stress at the cost of a 20% chance to gain the  Ill trait and will give the character the  Cannibal trait if they don't already have it.
Rulers who employ an Executioner court position.png Executioner can choose to make the execution public, which grants +15 Popular Opinion for 5 years in the realm capital and increase Control in all domain counties by +3 for each Aptitude level.
Adventurers who have the Long Pig Intrigue lifestyle perk can choose to turn the prisoner into a random amount of  Provisions.
If the character has a royal court and the  Arbitrary,  Deceitful,  Callous,  Sadistic or  Vengeful trait, executing a rival has a 25% chance to give the option to turn their skull into a pedestal item artifact. The offer is guaranteed if the prisoner was a nemesis. If the ruler does not have an Executioner court position.png Executioner it will lose -30  Opinion with the Personal Physician or Antiquarian.

Torture
Torturing a character will give the following:

The torturer loses −100  Piety unless their faith has the Exaltation of Pain tenet or they have the Divine Retribution perk
The torturer gains +100  Piety if their faith has the Exaltation of Pain tenet
The torturer loses −10  Stress if they have the  Sadistic trait
The torturer gains +10  Stress if they have the  Trusting trait
The torturer gains +100  Stress if they have the  Compassionate trait
The tortured character loses −75  Opinion for 50 years towards the torturing character
The tortured character gains the Recently Tortured modifier for 5 years giving  −0.75 Health
If the tortured character has or knows a secret it will have the option to reveal it to avoid torture
Tradition punishments
The following punishments are only available for adult characters if their culture has certain traditions:

Castration is available to cultures with the Amharic Highlanders, Court Eunuchs, Byzantine Traditions or Palace Politics traditions. If the prisoner has below 12 years he will gain the  Beardless Eunuch trait, otherwise it will gain the  Eunuch trait. The castrated character has a 66% chance to gain a major penalty to  Health and  Stress for 5 years unless the character employs a  Personal Physician, in which case the chance ranges from 50% to 15% depending on Aptitude. Castrating gives -50  Opinion for 50 years with the prisoner and -20  Opinion for 20 years with their close family and spouses.
Blinding is available to cultures with the Byzantine Traditions, Merciful Blindings or Palace Politics tradition. The prisoner gains the  Blind trait. Blinding gives -50  Opinion for 50 years with the prisoner and -20  Opinion for 20 years with their close family and spouses.
Maiming is available only to characters in a Duumvirate and will end the power sharing. Can choose to give the prisoner the  Blind,  Disfigured,  One-Legged or  Maimed. Males can also be castrated. If the co-emperor does it, they take all of the emperor's titles except the family nobly family one.
Sun trial
Sun trial is a punishment available to faiths with the Sun Worship tenet and only towards prisoners who are guilty of a crime. The characters will gain 100  Piety. The prisoner will either die or survive, and if they survive and have the same faith they will gain +3 Monthly Piety and +15 Same Faith Opinion for 15 years. The chance to survive depends on the terrain of the realm capital and the  Health of the prisoner.
