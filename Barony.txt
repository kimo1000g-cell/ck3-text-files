Barony
Jump to navigationJump to search
Please help with verifying or updating older sections of this article.
At least some were last verified for version 1.18.

This article is for the PC version of Crusader Kings 3 only.

A Barony is the lowest rank of landed titles and the only rank that is not playable (although they may be held by playable characters). Each county has 1-7 baronies, which will always be in the same realm as the county they're part of. Baronies may be sparsely populated or have a holding.

Capital
A barony can be three types of capital:

County capital.png The county capital is the primary barony of a county. Its holding can never be destroyed and is always owned by the ruler who holds the county title. County capitals allow the construction of an additional building and have +1  Fort Level. The county capital can be changed when the owner of the county also has another holding in the county.
County capital.png The duchy capital is the primary barony of a duchy. Duchy capitals have an additional building slot dedicated to the duchy building. Duchy capitals can be changed via the Relocate De jure Capital but if a duchy building was constructed it will be removed.
Realm capital.png The realm capital is the barony where a ruler and its court resides and is always inherited by the primary heir. The realm capital can only be moved once per lifetime unless it's the de jure capital of the primary title and cannot be moved during a war. It also produces an additional +1  Tax and is less likely to join Peasant Rabble factions. Each barony within the same de jure duchy as the realm capital will gain +10%  Levies.
Holdings

A holding
Each barony may have a holding, which represents the main settlement inside it and is required to be able to use the barony. Constructing a new holding will create a title for that barony, which may be held or given to another character. Baronies that are not a county capital can be granted to a Courtier; when constructing holdings in a Vassal's county, the holder of the County title will automatically receive the holding once construction is complete.

Different holding types will only provide  Taxes and  Levies if held by rulers with certain government types. Giving away a holding that does not provide anything does not give  Stress regardless of traits.

All holdings are constructed at level 1 and can be upgraded up to level 4. Constructing or upgrading a holding takes 5 years (before any bonuses/penalties).

Constructing a Castle, City or Temple holding requires 400  gold. If the county does not have a set of 1 Castle, 1 City and 1 Temple the missing holdings must be constructed first.
Upgrading a Castle, City, Temple or Temple Citadel holding to level 2 requires 550  gold.
Upgrading a Castle, City, Temple or Temple Citadel holding to level 3 requires 700  gold.
Upgrading a Castle, City, Temple or Temple Citadel holding to level 4 requires 850  gold.
Rulers that can't hold Tribe, Nomadic Settlement and Herder Pens holdings can turn them into Castle holdings at the cost of 200  gold if they have the same cultures and 500  gold if they have a different culture. For  Administrative governments the cost is increased unless the authority law is at the highest level. If the county is on the Siberian Permafrost the cost is increased to 2500 but also removes the permafrost.
If both a vassal and a liege have a government that can't hold Tribe, Nomadic Settlement or Herder Pens holdings, the liege can offer via a character interaction to pay for converting the holding. The liege will gain  Legitimacy, as well as  Influence if they government uses the resource.
New holdings cannot be constructed in Sahara.

Tribe
Tribe holdings can only be held directly or upgraded by rulers with  Tribal or  Wanua government. Tribe holdings cannot be constructed. Upgrading a Tribe holding costs 200  Gold and 400  Prestige and requires the  Plenary Assemblies innovation.

If the Khans of the Steppe Khans of the Steppe DLC is installed they will also give +35% County Fertility Growth. Rulers with  Tribal or  Wanua government can turn Nomadic Settlement and Herder Pens holdings into a Tribe holding at the cost of 50  Gold and 50  Prestige if the county has the same culture, 100  Prestige if the county has a different culture with the same  Heritage, and 300  Prestige if the county has a culture with a different  Heritage.

Nomadic Settlement
Khans of the Steppe	Available only with the Khans of the Steppe DLC enabled.
Nomadic Settlement is a holding type found throughout the Steppe region and will only provide their effects to rulers with  Nomadic government. Buildings cannot be constructed in a Nomadic Settlement. A Nomadic Settlement has the following effects:

 +5% Stationed Men-at-Arms Damage
 +50% Realm County Fertility
 +1 County Fertility
 +25 Plague Resistance
 +100% Hostile Raid Time
 Fort Level depends on the Nomadic Capital of their holder
Nomadic Settlements can be abandoned to a Herder at any point, turning them into Herder Pens.

Herder Pens
Khans of the Steppe	Available only with the Khans of the Steppe DLC enabled.
Herder Pens is a holding type present in all counties controlled by a character with  Herder government. Its only effects are giving the county +25  Plague Resistance and -8  Danger. They will also give +2  County Fertility Growth if the county's culture has the Pastoralists or Wolves of the Deep Steppe traditions.

If the county title is taken by a character with  Nomadic government, the holding is converted into a Nomadic Settlement. Other governments do not receive any benefit from owning Herder Pens.

Razing holdings
Khans of the SteppeDLC All Under Heaven.png	Available only with the Khans of the Steppe DLC or the All Under Heaven DLC enabled.
Rulers with  Meritocratic Khanate government can raze City, Temple and Tribe holdings held directly every 5 years to gain scaled  Gold and rulers with  Nomadic government can raze Castle holdings as well. Razing a holding downgrades all buildings by one level, removes them if they can't be downgraded, and if there are no buildings left razing the holding will turn it into a Nomadic Settlement. Special buildings cannot be destroyed and are retained if the holding becomes a Nomadic Settlement.

Winter

The Himalayas affected by winter while India is unaffected
All baronies that are not in the lower third of the map will suffer winter effects each year. Winter starts near the middle of October and its Severity slowly increases but it is not the same every year. How early and severely it affects a barony depends on its position on the map, with northmost and mountain baronies being affected first and most severely. Winters end at the beginning of March. Certain regiments of Men-at-Arms receive bonuses or penalties during Winter.

Plagues

The Black Death
A plague is an outbreak of a contagious disease trait that spreads across the map. If a plague is present within a ruler's domain they unlock the Isolate Capital and Enter Seclusion decisions (these increase the  Plague Resistance at a cost of other penalties). If a plague is present within the player's realm or the realm of a neighbor then a button will appear in the lower-right corner, showing all plagues.

Affected baronies have an Infection Rate that determines the penalties they get. The maximum Infection Rate is reduced by a percentage equal to the county's  Plague Resistance, which also reduced their spread.

If  Plague Resistance is above 10 plagues are 25% less likely to spread to or increase Infection Rate in the barony.
If  Plague Resistance is above 50 plagues are 50% less likely to spread to or increase Infection Rate in the barony.
If  Plague Resistance is above 80 plagues are 75% less likely to spread to or increase Infection Rate in the barony.
The number of buildings increase the chance for a plague to appear or spread in a barony, especially Building tradeport.png tradeports, Building market villages.png markets, Building guild halls.png guilds, or if the holding is a Holding city.png city. Plagues spread faster across coastal regions. They are less likely to spread to islands, with the exception of Great Britain. Once a plague ends the affected baronies will receive a  Development growth bonus for 5 years and will be 4 times more resistant to plagues for 50 years. Plagues cannot spread to and from Iceland.

When a barony is infected its county will lose  Development. The penalty scales inversely with the number of baronies so for example a county with 3 baronies will lose 3 times less  Development per infected barony than a county with 1 barony.

Each plague will have one of three levels of intensity, which affects their duration, number of baronies that will be affected, and chance for characters to get infected. The ruler who has the source of the outbreak within its domain will lose 20, 50 or 100  Legitimacy depending on intensity.  Development and later eras increase the chance for a plague to be of higher intensity.

An Apocalyptic  Bubonic Plague will always be named Black Death. Every ruler will be notified when it appears and when it spreads inside their borders. Where it can appear, how it spreads and whether it can appear multiple times depends on Game Rules. If the DLC Legends of the Dead.png Legends of the Dead DLC is installed there will be numerous unique events that can happen during Black Death.

If the DLC Legends of the Dead.png Legends of the Dead DLC is installed the map table will change appearance during a major epidemic. This behavior can be disabled in the settings menu.
