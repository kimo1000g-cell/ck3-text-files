Character modifiers
A modifier is an effect that adds various bonuses or penalties to a dynasty, house, character or county. Modifiers can be temporary or permanent. Characters can have an unlimited number of modifiers but only the first 10 will be displayed.

Warhorse
The warhorse is a unique modifier that can gradually increase in effects. It can be obtained as a result of travel,  Martial Lifestyle or Wandering lifestyle.png Wandering Lifestyle events. If the Royal Court DLC is installed it can also be obtained from royal court events. The modifier will initially grant the following bonuses:

+1  Prowess
+0.25 Monthly  Prestige
The day after the modifier is obtained an event will trigger to give the warhorse a name.

The modifier can be improved in two ways, both costing a small amount of  Gold and offering a choice of what horse talent to improve. The modifier can only be improved twice.

Every 275-400 days there is a 15% chance to gain the option to train the warhorse. If the Master of the Horse court position is being used its holder can be asked to do it instead, which removes the  Gold cost and grants a temporary +15  Opinion with the Master of the Horse but chooses the training at random. The event can only take place once.
25-30 years after obtaining the modifier the warhorse will start to die of old age and offer the option to breed it. Not breeding it will result in the loss of the modifier in 30-60 days.

Pets
Pets are character modifiers that last between 11 and 16 years. They can be inherited if a character dies before the pet but can also be lost through events. Both pets unlock a  Decision to pet them, have a hidden 33% chance to save the character when a Murder Scheme is executed against them, and have a very small chance to expose schemes targeting the character. There are three pet modifiers:

 Pet Cat grants +1 to  Intrigue and  Learning
 Pet Dog grants +1 to  Diplomacy and  Prowess and can receive additional events during  Hunt activities
Modifier bird positive.png Hunting Eagle grants +1 to  Martial and  Stewardship and can receive additional events during  Hunt activities
Pets can be obtained in multiple ways. One day after getting the pet the character will be given the option to input a name for the pet.

At any point the character may be given a pet from a consort, friend, lover or vassal and gain the option to keep it or sell it for a minor random amount of  Gold.
During childhood the character might encounter a pet and have the option to keep it and gain the modifier. Alternatively if the character has the Trait sadistic.png Sadistic trait it can kill the pet to gain +1  Learning.
A Pet Dog can be obtained via the Adopt Puppy and Adopt a Kennel Dog decisions.
If the Royal Court Royal Court DLC is installed a pet can be offered in certain court events.
If the DLC Wandering Nobles.png Wandering Nobles DLC is installed a pet can be offered as part of travel events.
A day after obtaining a pet an event will trigger to give the pet a name. While the character has a pet the following events can take place:

A vassal plays with the pet and gains +20  Opinion
The pet brings 2  Gold
The pet gives a temporary  Health Boost
The pet ruins a vassal's possessions, bringing the following options:
Pay the vassal and lose a moderate random amount of  Gold
Lose -20  Opinion with the vassal
If the character has two pets it might gain an event that grants -20  Stress
If the character has two pets they might fight. Within two weeks one on the following will happen:
75% chance a pet dies
15% chance both pets die
10% chance they get along and the character gains -10  Stress
If both DLC Tours and Tournaments.png Tours and Tournaments and Roads to Power Roads to Power DLCs are installed, there are two variants of the Modifier dog positive.png Pet Dog modifier that can be obtained for 25  Gold as part of adventurer random events. Both of them increase  Hunt success chance:

Beasthound gives +1  Martial and  Prowess and +0.1 Monthly Prestige
Scenthound gives +1  Diplomacy and  Learning and +0.1 Monthly Prestige
Royal Court	Available only with the Royal Court DLC enabled.
When a pet dies of old age, if the character has a Royal Court it will have the option to pay 10-20  Gold to gain a Small Wall Ornament artifact.
