Natural disasters
Natural disasters cannot happen in the first 10 years of a game. Afterwards, one of each natural disaster will take place every year. Selecting a disaster will display all affected counties with a flashing red border. Natural disasters are 2 times less likely to take place in the de jure capital of a  Duchy, and 4 times less likely if it's also the de jure capital of a  Kingdom. They are also 10 times less likely to take place in a region that already had a natural disaster.

Natural disaster situations affect the owners of counties where the disaster takes place, as well as any liege they have.

Every natural disaster has one of three levels of severity.

Every natural disaster situation has 3 phases.

Warning phases last 40 days; halfway through, every affected ruler will receive the option to gain 10  Stress and enter a regency while the situation lasts so that recovery contributions will cost 10% less. If the ruler is already in a regency the ruler will not gain  Stress.
Impact phases last 30 days. Every affected ruler will lose 50  Legitimacy if not Hegemon of China and 100 Mandate legitimacy.png Legitimacy if Hegemon of China and every location will lose  Control, will no longer increase in  Development, and will receive a modifier based on the situation's severity for 25 years. Every 7 days characters will die, every army located in the situation region has a 25-75% chance to be damaged and lose supplies (depending on its size), and the rulers affected will receive a random negative event. The map terrain will visibly change until the situation ends.
Recovery phases last for 15 years and give every affected ruler -0.25  Monthly Legitimacy. Affected rulers can pay various contributions to receive bonuses. The cost of each contribution depends on the number of counties affected, the severity of the disaster, the ruler's  Tax income and the innovations from their culture. If all mandatory contributions have been paid, the situation will end after another two months, and all penalties from the disaster will be removed. If the disaster affects multiple independent realms, each realm must pay the contributions separately to end the disaster within their own realm.

If a human player would be picked for death during a situation, both the player's character and a close family member, always the primary heir if there is one, will be at risk and the player will receive the following choices:

Try to survive together through a twice as difficult  Prowess challenge. Both characters will be wounded, and if the challenge is failed one of the characters will die.
Try to survive separately through a  Prowess challenge. Any character who fails the challenge will die.
Sacrifice yourself to save the family member. The player character will die, the family member will escape unharmed, and the dynasty will gain 250  Renown.
Sacrifice the family member to survive. The family member will die and the player character will escape unharmed; the player character will lose 350  Prestige and 250  Piety, and gain the  Craven trait.

Dynastic Cycle
DLC All Under Heaven.png	Available only with the All Under Heaven DLC enabled.
The Dynastic Cycle is present in all start dates. A character will become an interloper if it either owns de jure territory of Hegemony of China or they are located inside a barony that's both de jure part of Hegemony of China's and currently owned by them. They will become involved if they are either part of Hegemony of China or if they are a tributary who has Palace Access in their contract.

Phases of the Dynastic Cycle are called eras. In 1066 it starts in the Dynastic cycle phase advancement.png Advancement era, in 867 and 1178 it starts in the Dynastic cycle phase instability.png Tension era, and in 867 it also starts with 40% progress towards the Division era. The situation will alternate between Dynastic cycle phase instability.png Tension and either Dynastic cycle phase advancement.png Advancement or Dynastic cycle phase advancement.png Expansion. Which era will be picked depends on the Favored Era, which can be selected by the Hegemon every 20 years at the cost of 250  Influence. When an Dynastic cycle phase expansion.png Advancement or Dynastic cycle phase advancement.png Expansion era starts, the Hegemon can spend 225 Mandate legitimacy.png Legitimacy and 50  Opinion with every member of the Advancement, Expansion and Conservative movement to switch to the other era.

Every 10 years the hegemon and members of a political movement can add 50 Catalyst Progress towards or away from Advancement, Expansion, or Division. This costs 250  Influence and 200 Group power.png Power for their movement (Pro-Dynasty if the hegemon does it).

Division
The Division phase can be reached either through Catalysts during the Tension or Conquest phase, if the hegemon owns less than 30% of China's de jure territory or reaches the Mandate legitimacy 0.png Illegitimate Ruler legitimacy level, or if the Hegemony of China title is destroyed. When the Division phase is reached all  Hegemony titles owned by the ruler of China title are destroyed, the former hegemon suffers an immediate succession, and all its subjects become independent. Every  Kingdom title that is part of it is also destroyed if its holder has less than 100  Movement Power and every  Empire title that is part of it is also destroyed if its holder has less than 350  Movement Power. The Silk Road situation will enter the Hardship phase in every region and the Attend Children's Examination decision will become unavailable.

As a result of the changes, every previously involved ruler will become an interloper. All interlopers will receive -20%  Taxes and +100%  Legitimacy from completing great projects; will gain  Legitimacy if they appoint characters with Merit Rank 1-4 as governors, councilors and court positions; and will unlock the Consolidate Land Under Heaven casus belli. The only way for the Division phase to end is by claiming the Mandate of Heaven, which is available to all independent rulers who control more than half of the de jure counties of  Hegemony of China. Political movements
In the Dynastic Cycle situation all subjects of China are divided among four sides, called Political Movements. Political Movements give various effects depending on the current era. A subject can change their Political Movement every 5 years at the cost of 250  Influence. The hegemon can favor a movement every 20 years at the cost of 250  Influence and 25 Progress towards Dynastic cycle phase chaos.png Division.

Every subject of China involved in the Dynastic Cycle has a Personal Movement Power, which is increased by the following:

Age
Title rank
Merit rank
Level of devotion
Level of influence
Men-at-arms

Estate upgrades

Being a diarch
Every Political Movement has a Movement Power, which is the sum of Personal Movement Power of all members. Reaching 800 Movement Power unlocks for every member Dismiss Hook and Request Permission to Raid interactions and the Raid Estate scheme.

Every Political Movement has a leader, who unlocks the Make Movement Petition decision. When the leader dies the character with most Personal Movement Power becomes the new leader but they will be replaced if they are defeated in a Activity debate.png Debate activity.

The Silk Road

The Silk Road situation showing the movement of innovations
DLC All Under Heaven.png	Available only with the All Under Heaven DLC enabled.
The Silk Road situation affects all rulers with their capital in one of the situation regions. There are 6 situation regions, connected in the following order: Occident < Transcaspia and India < Central Asia and Tibet < China. Every region has an innovation assigned and every 12 years a random innovation discovered by the Han culture will be assigned to the China situation and all existing innovations will move westwards. These innovations can be researched normally by cultures with Chinese heritage or the Sinophilic tradition as well as if a ruler of that culture controls the region's hub while the innovation is present in the region. Other rulers can attempt to unlock these innovations by taking the Visit a Silk Road Market decision and passing the skill challenge.

How each ruler is affected depends on the phase in their region and their participant group.

Steppe seasons

The Great Steppe regions before any expansion
Khans of the Steppe	Available only with the Khans of the Steppe DLC enabled.
Steppe seasons are situations that affect characters with  Nomadic government present in certain locations if The Great Steppe game rule was enabled. The character with the highest  Herd involved in a situation will be considered the Gurkhan, who gains access to the Nomad authority 5.png Unrestricted Dominance law and the Establish Paiza System decision.

There are 5 steppe season situations, depending on where the character's Nomadic Capital is located. Every steppe season situation covers multiple regions, and different regions may be affected by different situation phases. The regions can be expanded via the Expand the Steppes decision.
