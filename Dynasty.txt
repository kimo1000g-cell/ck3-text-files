A reputable dynasty
A Dynasty represents an unbroken noble line descending from the same Landed character who founded the dynasty. Each dynasty is made up of multiple Houses working towards bringing Renown upon the dynasty. A dynasty can be renamed by the Dynasty Head at any point.

Houses
Each dynasty will have a Founding House, usually of the same name as the dynasty, which is the first house of that dynasty. Ruling members of a dynasty may choose to create a Cadet Branch, a new house under the dynasty. Each house has its own name and motto, usually inspired by the location in which they are founded and the founding character, which can be changed at will by the House Head. They will also have a unique coat of arms that has two quarters copied from the dynasty coat of arms.

If a House Head demands the religious conversion of a house member and the member refuses, it will result in the creation of a new Cadet Branch.

Characters are born into father's house in Patrilineal marriage, or mother's house in Matrilineal marriage.

House head
The House Head is the leader of a house. House Heads have the power to call house members to war at no cost and demand that they convert to their Faith and get a  Weak Hook on all newly born house members and monthly prestige from living and landed members of their house. If faith has the Legitimization doctrine for bastards they can also legitimize them and if faith has the Must be Approved doctrine for divorces but no head of faith, the House Head must approve of divorces. When a House Head dies, their Primary Heir becomes the next House Head.

When a character creates a Cadet Branch, they become its House Head, and free from the direct influence of their old House Head unless they were the Dynasty Head.

Dynasty head
The most powerful House Head of a dynasty will be the Dynasty Head. If another House Head becomes 10% stronger than the current Dynasty Head it will take its place. Most of the time Dynasty Head is calculated by military strength, but if all House Heads have  Administrative,  Celestial,  Meritocratic,  Meritocratic Khanate or  Ritsuryō government then it will be calculated by powerful family rating. House heads with Adventurer.png Adventurer government cannot become dynasty heads although custom characters will start as one.

The Dynasty Head gains Monthly Prestige from living and landed members of their dynasty. The Dynasty Head can always disinherit and also has access to the following interactions with dynasty members of the same faith:

Blocs

A bloc
DLC All Under Heaven.png	Available only with the All Under Heaven DLC enabled.
Blocs are defensive alliances between rulers from different houses present in Japan and also block members from going to war against each other. Only house heads can leave blocs or ask to join another bloc. If a house leaves a bloc all its members will gain a 5-year truce will all members of houses from their previous bloc. Leaving a bloc will also give -30% House Relation with the bloc's leading house and -15 Opinion with its house head. Leaving a house bloc is free but joining a house bloc costs 100  Prestige. A member who joins a bloc cannot leave it for 10 years.

Every bloc has a leader, which determines the name of the bloc. Its house head decides if another house can join the bloc, can remove houses from the bloc at will, and is the only character who can create factions. Only the bloc leader can create factions against the top liege and all bloc house members will automatically join the faction. A bloc leader cannot join another bloc but can always pass leadership to another house of their bloc, which will give +30% House Relation with it. Another house head within the bloc can spend 100  Influence if the government uses the resource or 200  Prestige if it does not to ask for leadership as long as the leader is not their liege if they are landed while the leader is not, have a higher title rank than them, are two levels of fame above them, or have a higher level than them in the same aspiration. The existing leader will have to accept and they will always refuse if the asking house head has a different government but the asking house head can spend a Hook to force them to accept it.

Every bloc has a Cohesion, which ranges from 0 to 100 and gives bonuses to all members depending on the leader's House Attribute. It is affected by the relation between all member houses and decreased by the number of landed rulers in the bloc, especially if their government is not the same as the leader's. It is also increased by +0.25 by every house that has the same family attribute as the leading house and decreased by -0.25 for every house that has a different family attribute from the leading house and by -0.1 each month by every member house. If Cohesion reaches 0 the bloc will be disbanded.

Only house heads with  Ritsuryō or  Sōryō government can create or join a bloc or ask to become its leader, but their house members will be part of it regardless of government. If the leader changes government to something other than  Ritsuryō or  Sōryō the bloc will keep using the previous House Attribute for bonuses but Cohesion will still be reduced from members not having the same House Attribute.

There are 5 blocs in every start date. Every bloc starts with 50 Cohesion.

Disinheriting
Disinheriting is a character interaction that will give the targeted character the Trait disinherited.png Disinherited trait and a permanent -75  Opinion. It can be reversed at any point at the cost of 75  Renown. Disinheriting requires the target to be a vassal and is not available towards characters with a  Strong Hook. Reversing a disinheritance costs 75  Renown.

Dynasty Heads can disinherit any member of their dynasty for the following cost:

Free if the targeted dynasty member has the  Disputed Heritage or  Bastard trait.
75  Renown and 150  Prestige if the targeted dynasty member is Unlanded
150  Renown and 300  Prestige if the targeted dynasty member is Heir to a title
225  Renown and 900  Prestige if the targeted dynasty member is Landed or part of an Administrative family
50  Legitimacy if the targeted dynasty member is not the Dynasty Head's child or primary title heir
100  Legitimacy if the targeted dynasty member is the Dynasty Head's child but not primary title heir
200  Legitimacy if the targeted dynasty member is the Dynasty Head's primary title heir
20  Stress if dynasty head has the  Compassionate or  Forgiving trait
20  General Opinion for 5 years
DLC Fate of Iberia.png	Available only with the Fate of Iberia DLC enabled.
All dynasty members, including the Dynasty Head, can disinherit their own children with the last Coterie legacy track.png Coterie legacy. This disinheritance does not cost  Renown but it gives -30  Opinion with all vassals.

Renown

Dynasty legacies
Renown is a resource accumulated by a dynasty and is used for the most powerful Dynasty Head interactions, unlocking legacies and increasing a dynasty's level of Splendor. Each living dynasty member will add Renown to the dynasty, and Prestige to its Dynasty Head and House Head.

Level of Splendor
Firstly, all renown earned by a dynasty counts towards its Level of Splendor, which determines how well-known the dynasty is in the eyes of the world. Having a high level also makes it much easier to arrange marriages, especially with Dynasties with a Lower Level of Splendor. The Level of Splendor will only decrease if a player character attempts suicide. Peasant Dynasties start at Base Origins. The alliance acceptance bonus does not apply if both characters are part of the same dynasty.

The first dynasty legacy costs 250  Renown to unlock and each one afterwards costs 500 more than the previous one.

Characters within the same dynasty and especially close family have an  Opinion bonus with each other. However the bonus is only given to public connections, secret family relations do not give any bonuses. In scenarios where a character has multiple relations to another all relevant modifiers are applied.
